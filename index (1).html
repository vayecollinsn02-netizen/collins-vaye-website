The Journey

From Sanniquellie to Carnegie Mellon

First in my family to attend university. First Ph.D. from my high school. First to negotiate his way into education by selling meat from a tray at age 12. Now: making sure I'm not the last.

Born during Liberia's 14-year civil war, I learned early that education was not a given — it was something you had to create for yourself. At 12, in Sanniquellie City, I approached a private school registrar with a proposal: a small down payment and a promise to pay the rest by year's end. They said yes. I balanced a large tray of meat on my head, walked through the city selling, and paid off my debt in small increments.

That was my first lesson in educational reform: Create your own opportunity.

First in family to attend university
First Ph.D. graduate from his high school
First to negotiate his way into education at age 12

Now at Carnegie Mellon University, I lead the Learning Sciences for Innovators Project across 12 African countries, partnering with 400+ EdTech entrepreneurs through the Mastercard Foundation. African educators don't need saving; they need amplification. My research documents how African institutions pioneer contextual innovations often overlooked by global narratives.
